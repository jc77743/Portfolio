Initial Waste Audit App

Project Overview

Zero Waste Pennsylvania (ZWP)
Project Manager and Full Stack Engineer
Pittsburgh, PA
Spring 2013

Opportunity

  • ZWP is a non profit that helps businesses and communities reduce their waste
  • ZWP came to CMU looking for assistance in how technology could improve their offerings
  • After shadowing ZWP employees on an engagement, we saw that their "Initial Waste Audit" was their biggest pain point
  • We developed a mobile application that automized their process, making reporting take hours, not weeks
  • Our application greatly increased their sales and the value of their mission

Gathering Requirements

  • We began by asking ZWP about what they wanted to improve on
  • We asked to shadow them on an engagement
  • We recorded them, and used Businss Process Modeling to map their workflow
  • We found the biggest paint point was their initial proposal to potential clients
  • This was all done by hand in Excel, so it lacked consistency, quality control, and often took weeks
  • We quickly developed an MVP to show how we could automate and standarize the process

Developing in Ruby on Rails

  • We used Ruby on Rails because our whole team was familiar with it
  • We liked that Rails had the gems that we would need, and was sustainable and robust
  • We used gems to allow photo to be uploaded to Dropbox, web pages to be downloaded as pdfs, and items to be edited with ajax
  • We used a Postgress database and customized Bootstrap for styling

Unique User Experience Insights

  • There would be one employee at ZWP who used our application
  • We met every week with him to show him our progress on that sprint
  • We customized every feature to his liking, doing plenty of A/B testing and user walkthroughs
  • We had him go on a mock engagement twice to test the app in real world settings
  • He preferred a tablet over a mobile device or PC, so practiced 'Tablet-first' design

My Role

  • In our team of 3, I worked as our project manager and as a full stack developer
  • I led most of our client meetings, distilling requirements from our client's feedback
  • From this project, I developed my first end-to-end app, and found I was really interested in user experience
  • I enjoyed doing work for a real entity, and contributing to a non-profit's mission
