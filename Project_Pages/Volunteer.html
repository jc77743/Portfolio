Overview

I've been doing volunteer work and community service since a young age. I've found it to be a great way to give back, meet new people, and learn more about the places I've lived in.

Minds Matter NYC Sophomore Mentor Program

  • I started volunteeting with this org October of 2015 after finding out about it through PwC
  • I co-mentor a high achieving high school sophomore from a low income family
  • We meet once a week to work on modules set by Minds Matter, relating to the college admission process, public speaking and debates, and optimizing your time in high school
  • I enjoy this because it relates to the work I did as an RA and has helped me learn more about what it's like to grow up in New York

Outreach 360 International Volunteer Organization

  • My freshmen year at CMU, I joined this orgnaization that went to the Dominican Republic to teach English to local children
  • Throughout the year we fundraised ($14,000), learned more about the country, and crafted our curriculum
  • We spent one week in March in a small village, with limited amenities, teaching over 100 kids
  • This was a great opportunity for me to see the "real" side of a country mostly seen from a tourist's perspective, and practice my Spanish

CMU Leonard Gelfand Center

  • I volunteered for 2 years as a tutor at a local middle school in Pittsburgh, mentoring and helping out at risk teenagers
  • We met twice a week to work one-on-one with students, helping them with homework and acting as role models
  • I enjoyed this experience because it was a great way to get out of the CMU bubble and learn more about Pittsburgh
