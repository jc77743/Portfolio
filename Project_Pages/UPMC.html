UPMC Mobile Prototype

Project Overview

UPMC Technology Development Center
User Experience Lead
Pittsburgh, PA
Spring 2015

Opportunity

  • My team and I worked with UPMC's Technology Development Center (TDC) to modernize their system for paying study participants
  • Our analysis revealed that it was not mobile friendly, did not give intuitive cues, and was generall inefficient
  • We worked with a member of the TDC to team, creating a prototype to demonstrate how our recommendations would add value

Determining Pain Points

  • We did a full stack analysis, working from the ERDs to the user interface
  • We found that the database wasn't in 3rd normal form, the technology stack wasn't scalable to mobile, and the user experience was outdated and unintuitive
  • We felt creating a prototype would be the best way to communicate our recommendations

Developing a Proof of Concept

  • We decided to build our prototype in Ruby on Rails as our research showed this would be a viable enterprise level framework
  • We did research next on how our favorite mobile apps displayed large data sets (>1k items per user)
  • Using our knowledge of best practices, we showed our client low and high fidelity prototypes after 1 week
  • In the next 2 sprints, we completed our POC, using Card-based design and Ajax for seamless interactions

My Role

  • As the lead UX designer, I lead the research, creation of low and high fidelity prototypes, and development of our POC
  • I furthered my skills in working with a client and and presenting and providing recommendations
  • I enjoyed working to add real world value to a product through my creativity and programming skills
