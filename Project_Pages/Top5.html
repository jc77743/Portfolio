Project Overview

Project Manager and Full Stack Engineer
Pittsburgh, PA
Fall 2014

Opportunity

  • We wanted to change the way people interacted with restaurants and ordering
  • Too many times we found ourselves not knowing what would be dish would be good
  • Yelp, Foursquare, etc. didn't have a way to sort by dish, or view which was best
  • Every week we had to present to a panel of "investors" (our investors) how our app contributed to the world
  • We used data derived from user feedback to ensure we were always adding value

Making Data Driven Decisions

  • We spent countless hours user testing, in restaurants, to simulate real world condition
  • We did A/B testing for things such as the color scheme and how buttons worked
  • We'd give users a task with no instructions and watch and record them
  • We compiled this information into Personas to better understand our data
  • All this data allowed us to only add features that would enhance that the app

Using Agile

  • We used agile to maximize the effectives of our work
  • We started each meeting with a standup
  • Each week we'd present our progress that sprint, including justifying our backlog and future items
  • We used Pivotal Tracker to govern our sprints

Developing with MEAN Stack

  • We used MongoDB, Node.js, Express and Angularjs with Jade for our final iteration
  • MEAN was better for interacting with APIs and doing Mobile First Design
  • MEAN was fun because it was lightweight and something new and challenging

My Role

  • Since we were a small team (4), I got to experience being a project manager, UX designer and full stack engineer
  • I created and led most of our presentations, aligning our features with their value to users
  • I loved learning more about MEAN stack, espeically Angular
  • This was my first exposure to having large amounts of user data to inform our features
  • Two of my teammates were from CMU's campus in Qatar, so I was able to work on my cross culture collaboration skills
