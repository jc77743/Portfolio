Opportunity

  • We wanted to change the way people interacted with restaurants and ordering
  • Too many times we found ourselves not knowing what would be dish would be good
  • Yelp, Foursquare, etc. didn't have a way to sort by dish, or view which was best
  • Every week we had to present to a panel of "investors" (our investors) how our app contributed to the world
  • We used data derived from user feedback to ensure we were always adding value

Solution

Stack
We used MongoDB, Node.js, Express and Angularjs with Jade for our final iteration. Original, we started with Ruby on Rails and a Postgres database. We switched to MEAN because it could better handle our API, was better for mobile first design, and was something new and challenging.

Iterations
We used Agile Methodolgy to govern our sprints, deciding each week what we accomplish, and then starting each meeting with a standup to discuss what we would speficially get done. We used Pivotal Tracker to record our progress.

User Experience & Research
Since our app only existed to enhance restaurant goers' experiences, much of our time was spent doing user testing and research. We developed the app using Mobile First Design to make sure we were catering to the ideal experience. We did user walkthroughs very frequently, and A/B testing of features as we built them. To help understand and visualize our uses, we developed Personas to see what demographics liked what. Our final app was greatly shaped by our research, allowing us to make data driven decisions that would add value to each user.
