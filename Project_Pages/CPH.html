Copenhagen: Highlights and Attractions

Project Overview

CMU Communication Design Fundamentals
Author, Illustrator, Traveler
Copenhagen, Denmark
Spring 2015

Opportunity

  • For my commnication design class, we were tasked with created a book with all original content and photos
  • I had the priveldge of traveling to Denmark that semester, and I was really excited to share my favorite attractions and love of exploring new places

The Processs

  • I wanted to portray the ambiance and vibes of Copenhagen
  • I drew from typefaces, colors and styles I saw in the buildings and people around me
  • I started by creating a mood board
  • I iterated constantly on each page, going from a uniform design to each location having its own flavor

My Takeaways

  • This was a really fun project, as there were few parameters or checkpoints
  • I grew a lot as a designer from this, as I learned to explore my own creativity and iterate constantly from my own thoughts
  • I approached this at first as a web design
  • We used Pivotal Tracker to govern our sprints

Developing with MEAN Stack

  • We used MongoDB, Node.js, Express and Angularjs with Jade for our final iteration
  • MEAN was better for interacting with APIs and doing Mobile First Design
  • MEAN was fun because it was lightweight and something new and challenging

My Role

  • Since we were a small team (4), I got to experience being a project manager, UX designer and full stack engineer
  • I created and led most of our presentations, aligning our features with their value to users
  • I loved learning more about MEAN stack, espeically Angular
  • This was my first exposure to having large amounts of user data to inform our features
  • Two of my teammates were from CMU's campus in Qatar, so I was able to work on my cross culture collaboration skills
  • My teammates were: Cindy Zeng, Ali Naqi, and Noshin Nisa
